Yerebatan Sarnıcı - Basilica Cistern

Explore the ancient underground marvel in Istanbul.

Overview

The Yerebatan Sarnıcı, or Basilica Cistern, is a fascinating ancient underground water reservoir located in the heart of Istanbul, Turkey. Built in the 6th century during the reign of Byzantine Emperor Justinian I, it served as a crucial water supply for the Great Palace of Constantinople.

Architecture

The cistern is an architectural marvel featuring a vast space supported by a forest of marble columns. There are 336 columns arranged in 12 rows, and each column is 9 meters in height. The columns are thought to be repurposed from earlier Roman structures, adding to the historical significance of the site.

Medusa Heads

One of the intriguing features of the Basilica Cistern is the two Medusa heads located at the northwest corner of the cistern. These heads, one upside down and the other on its side, are believed to be repurposed from Roman sculptures and add a mysterious touch to the site.
