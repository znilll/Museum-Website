Beylerbeyi Palace

Explore the grandeur and history of Beylerbeyi Palace, a symbol of Ottoman architecture and luxury.

Overview

Beylerbeyi Palace, located on the Asian side of Istanbul, served as an imperial summer residence during the Ottoman period. Built in the 19th century, the palace boasts stunning architecture, exquisite interiors, and scenic views of the Bosphorus.

Architecture

The palace reflects the opulence of Ottoman architecture with its intricate designs, majestic domes, and ornate furnishings. Visitors can admire the well-preserved rooms, halls, and gardens that showcase the artistic and cultural heritage of the era.

Bosphorus Views

One of the highlights of Beylerbeyi Palace is its breathtaking views of the Bosphorus. The palace's waterfront location allows visitors to enjoy panoramic scenes of the strait, making it a picturesque destination.

Plan Your Visit

If you're planning to visit Beylerbeyi Palace, here are some essential details:

  • Location: Beylerbeyi Mahallesi, Abdullahaga Cd., 34676 Üsküdar/İstanbul, Turkey
  • Opening Hours: 09:00 AM to 05:00 PM (Closed on Mondays and Thursdays)
  • Admission: There is an entrance fee. Check the official website for the latest pricing information.
