Istanbul Archaeology Museums

Discover the rich history and cultural heritage at the Istanbul Archaeology Museums.

Overview

The Istanbul Archaeology Museums consist of three main museums: the Archaeological Museum, the Museum of the Ancient Orient, and the Museum of Islamic Art. Together, they house a vast collection of artifacts from various civilizations, providing a comprehensive journey through history.

Collections

Explore the diverse collections that span from ancient Mesopotamia and Anatolia to classical Greek and Roman civilizations. The museums showcase artifacts such as sculptures, pottery, coins, and more, offering visitors a glimpse into the rich cultural tapestry of the region.

Museum Building

The Istanbul Archaeology Museums are housed in a 19th-century neoclassical building, designed by the architect Alexander Vallaury. The building itself is a historical landmark and adds to the overall charm of the museum experience.

Plan Your Visit

If you're planning to visit the Istanbul Archaeology Museums, here are some essential details:

  • Location: Osman Hamdi Bey Yokuşu, Gulhane, 34122 Fatih/İstanbul, Turkey
  • Opening Hours: 09:00 AM to 05:00 PM (Closed on Mondays)
  • Admission: There is an entrance fee. Check the official website for the latest pricing information.
