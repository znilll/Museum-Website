Ephesus - Ancient City

Explore the historical marvels of Ephesus, an ancient city that holds a rich heritage and architectural wonders.

History

Ephesus, located in present-day Turkey, was an ancient Greek city that later became a major Roman city. It played a crucial role in various periods, known for its grand architecture, library, and the Temple of Artemis, one of the Seven Wonders of the Ancient World.

Archaeological Site

Visitors to Ephesus can explore the well-preserved archaeological site that includes iconic structures like the Library of Celsus, the Great Theatre, the Temple of Hadrian, and the Terrace Houses. These structures provide insights into the daily life and grandeur of the ancient city.

Library of Celsus

The Library of Celsus, a remarkable structure built to honor Roman Senator Tiberius Julius Celsus Polemaeanus, stands as one of the most impressive remnants. Its façade, reconstructed from the original pieces, showcases the architectural mastery of the time.
